Japanese Christmas Cake

Description

This traditional Japanese Christmas cake recipe is a favorite of cookbook author, recipe developer, and Allrecipes Allstar Lisa Childs, who makes it every year for the holidays. It's a super-light, delicious sponge cake layered with fresh whipped cream and strawberries.

Ingredients

  • 6 large eggs, separated and at room temperature
  • 22 tablespoons white sugar, divided
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 1/2 cups cake flour, sifted
  • 2 tablespoons avocado or grapeseed oil
  • 2 tablespoons whole milk
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 3 tablespoons orange liqueur, such as Grand Marnier®
  • 3 cups whipping cream
  • 1/2 to 3/4 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 tablespoon vanilla extract
  • 1 pound fresh strawberries, sliced (3 cups)

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 320°F, grease an 8-inch cake pan, and line it with parchment paper extending above the edges.
  2. Beat egg yolks with 10 tablespoons of sugar until pale and thick, then clean the beaters.
  3. Whip egg whites with 6 tablespoons of sugar until glossy, medium-stiff peaks form.
  4. Gently fold one-third of the egg white mixture into the egg yolk mixture, then fold in the remaining two-thirds without deflating the batter.
  5. Stir together salt and flour, then sift and fold the flour mixture into the batter in two stages until fully combined.
  6. Warm oil and milk, then fold into the batter until well combined, keeping it airy.
  7. Pour the batter into the prepared pan and gently tap it on the countertop to remove air bubbles.
  8. Bake the cake until golden brown and a thermometer reads at least 190°F, then invert onto a wire rack and let cool.
  9. Make simple syrup by mixing hot water, liqueur, and 6 tablespoons of sugar until dissolved, then let it cool.
  10. Whip cream, powdered sugar, and vanilla in a food processor until stiff peaks form.
  11. Slice the cooled cake into three 1-inch layers, brush each with simple syrup, and place one layer on a cake plate.
  12. Spread 3/4 cup of whipped cream on the first cake layer, top with a third of the strawberries, and another 3/4 cup of whipped cream; then repeat with the second and third cake layers.
  13. Frost the top and sides of the cake with whipped cream and garnish with the remaining strawberries.
  14. Chill the cake for 1 to 4 hours before serving, then cut into 10 slices.
